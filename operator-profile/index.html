The one weird field

Birth date, time, and place. Yes, really.

Your exact birth data goes into four independent readings: Western astrology (your natal chart), Vedic astrology (the sidereal chart, a second and older sky), the I Ching (the 64 gates, the developmental layer), and Human Design (type, authority, profile, centers, channels, incarnation cross). Each one gets a real, full read on its own terms — not a glance.

Then our synthesis engine lines all four up and finds where they converge — the places where four systems, run independently, land on the same truth about you. That convergence is what makes it into your profile. Where they don't agree, we drop it.

That's also why the birth time has to be exact. Guess it, and at least two of the four calculate wrong — the wrong house, the wrong gate — and the convergence breaks before it starts. If you don't know it off the top of your head, it's usually right there on your birth certificate.

Before you order

Why do you need my exact birth time?

Skip it and the read goes soft. There's no "close enough" version, so we don't offer one.

Isn't this just a personality test with extra steps?

A personality test hands you a label and stops. This hands you the place that label is costing you money right now, plus at least five things to do about it this week. If you want another label, plenty of places sell those.

What if I don't believe in any of this?

You don't have to, to check it. Page one is the operating contradiction — the exact place your wiring and your business are fighting. Hold it up against your actual week. If it's not true, don't bother reading the rest.

What if my wiring doesn't have much to say?

Some wirings are naturally more open and produce fewer hard instructions than others. Every document we build still ships at least five. What changes is the mix, never the number.
