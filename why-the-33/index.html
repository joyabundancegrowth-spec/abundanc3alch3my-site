The name

Why the 33?

The 3s where the E's should be aren't a typo, and they aren't a gimmick. They're a choice. Here's the whole of it.

Abundance→Abundanc3
Alchemy→Alch3my
The short answer

We put a number in the name. On purpose.

Two E's became two 3s — once in Abundanc3, again in Alch3my. Say it out loud and it's the same name. Read it, and there's a number sitting inside the word.

Three, and three again. Thirty-three. That's the point.

First, one 3

Three is the number of making.

One is the idea. Two is the tension — an idea meeting the world, a pair, a pull in two directions. Three is what they produce: the made thing.

It's also the first shape that stands on its own. Three points make a triangle — the first structure that holds its own weight. Creation and stability in a single digit. Seeing, tension, and the thing that gets built. That's the whole practice in one number.

Then, two

Doubled, it stops being personal.

33

A single 3 makes things for itself. Put two together and the number changes register.

In numerology, 33 is a master number — the one they call the Master Teacher. Same creative force, raised to a calling: making things that lift other people, not just yourself.

The tradition frames it as the seer and the builder fused into one — vision and construction in the same pair of hands. We didn't pick that meaning to fit. It was already sitting in the number. One person who sees it and builds it — that's the entire practice.

We didn't invent this

Three has carried weight for a very long time.

Set the meaning aside for a second. These are just facts — humanity has marked three, and thirty-three, as significant across thousands of years and traditions that never met.

The threes of the sacred

The Christian Trinity. The Hindu Trimurti — creator, preserver, destroyer. Buddhism's Three Jewels. Again and again, the divine gets organized in threes.

33 at the top

The 33rd degree is the highest in Scottish Rite Freemasonry. The human spine forms with 33 vertebrae. Christ is traditionally said to have been 33.

Written in on purpose

Dante built two of his three books at 33 cantos each. The Bible records King David reigning 33 years. The number keeps getting chosen — deliberately.

We're not the first to feel the weight of it. We just made it visible.

Why spell it this way

The word you use every day is the one that does the work.

You can carry an intention quietly. Or you can put it in the one word that gets typed, said, and read a hundred times a day.

We chose the loud version. Every time you see Abundanc3 Alch3my, the number is right there in the letters — a small, deliberate doorway. Some people read straight past it. Some stop. If you stopped, that was the whole idea.

Straight, since you're wondering

We're not claiming the spelling changes your luck. Numbers don't run the business.

But the words you choose to live inside shape what you notice. We chose ours to point straight at what we do — see it, then build it. That's not superstition. That's design.

If the doorway did something

Walk through it.

The whole practice is about the gap between who you are and how you operate — named, then closed. The name is just the first place we tell the truth about it.
