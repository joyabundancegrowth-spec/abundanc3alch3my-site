Why this exists

You don't need more advice. You need to see the pattern.

You've built something real. But you're standing inside it, and from inside you can't see the one thing that's actually costing you. So you keep reaching for more — more traffic, more content, more discipline.

Most advisors hand you a longer to-do list. The Clarity Intensive does the opposite. It finds the single pattern underneath the noise, proves it to you, and tells you exactly what to do about it — in order.

How it works

Two sessions. One week apart.

The week in between isn't a scheduling quirk — it's processing time, for both of us. Both sessions are recorded with your permission, because the map gets built from re-listening, not memory.

Session 01 · 90 min
The business audit

Where is the business quietly losing money or energy — the stuff that's invisible because you're standing inside it? Four zones, in order:

›The funnel. Where people fall off between finding you and buying — and whether you actually know, or you're guessing.

›Underused assets. The thing that already works, lands, converts — that no one's building on.

›The money. The costs and leaks you couldn't put a number to right now if I asked.

›The founder bottleneck. Where the business only moves when you move.

Session 02 · 90 min
The person audit

How are you wired — and where does that collide with how the business needs to run? Same rule: we answer the obvious ones anyway.

›How you decide. Gut, logic, waiting for a sign, polling everyone — and what you rush versus what you sit on too long.

›Your real energy. When you do your best work, and how much draining work you're still carrying anyway.

›What you avoid. The task you put real energy into not doing. Usually the tell.

›How you're built. A read on your wiring — used only to confirm the patterns we already found, never to lead.

The deliverable

The Clarity Map

A written document you keep — not notes, not a recording. A map. Five parts:

01
The diagnosis

One paragraph. The single pattern underneath all the problems, named in a sentence. Not a list — the one thing.

02
The evidence

Proof the pattern is real — one instance from the business, one from you, side by side. So it lands, instead of reading like a horoscope.

03
Three priorities

Ranked. The three moves that close the gap fastest — by leverage, not urgency. Which one first, and why the others wait.

04
The 90-day roadmap

Three phases, about 30 days each. The order of operations — what each phase accomplishes. The sequencing logic, not a task list.

05
The close

Plainly: what happens if nothing changes, versus what's available if this gap closes. So the decision in front of you is clear.

Yours to keep

The map is self-contained. You can take it and run it yourself — or hand it to me to build. Either way, it's yours.

Do I need my numbers ready?

No. Come as you are. The gaps in what you know are part of the diagnosis — the number you can't name is often where the leak is.

How is this different from consulting?

A consultant hands you a deck of recommendations. This hands you the one pattern, the evidence, and the order to act — and, if you want it, the person who'll build it.

What if we don't find anything?

We will. The zone that feels like "nothing to see here" is usually exactly where the gap is hiding. That's the point of asking the obvious questions anyway.

Is it really just two sessions?

Two sessions with you. The map itself is built in the days between and after — re-listening, connecting the two audits, and writing it down. That's where the real work happens.
